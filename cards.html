Q3 is active — you're set

Your Discover 5% categories are activated — gas, EV charging, transit, flights and drugstores earn 5% through September 30, on a shared $1,500 cap. Nothing more to do until the categories reset October 1.

Heads-up: your Discover becomes a Capital One card on July 27. Same account, same card number, same rotating 5%, same Cash Back Match — you'll just log in through the Capital One app from then on.

The one rule that matters

Citi Custom Cash only pays 5% on one category per billing cycle — whichever eligible category you spent the most in, capped at the first $500. It picks automatically, so splitting between gas and groceries silently halves the bonus. Pick one lane and stay in it. Groceries is the right lane this quarter, because Discover already covers gas and drugstores through September.

The cards themselves

Discover It — Q3 2026
  • 5% gas stations, EV charging, public transportation, flights, drugstores
  • 1% everything else
The $1,500 cap is shared across all five categories combined, not per category — spend it where the alternative card is weakest. Activation window runs through September 30. Becomes a Capital One card on July 27, 2026; rotating categories and Cash Back Match both survive the move, and you'll gain 5% on Capital One Travel and Entertainment bookings afterward.
Citi Custom Cash
  • 5% on your single top eligible category each cycle, first $500
  • 1% everything else, and everything past the cap
Eligible: restaurants, gas, groceries, drugstores, home improvement, select travel, transit, streaming, fitness clubs, live entertainment. You don't choose — it auto-picks your highest-spend category. $25/cycle max bonus. Citi closed this card to new applications in May 2026, so if you ever cancel it you cannot get it back.
Chase Sapphire Preferred
  • 5x travel booked through Chase Travel
  • 3x dining (incl. bars & lounges), select streaming, online groceries
  • 3x gas & EV charging — added June 2026, year-round, no cap
  • 3x vacation rentals: Airbnb, Vrbo — added June 2026
  • 2x all other travel
  • 1x everything else
$95 annual fee, unchanged by the June 15 2026 refresh. That refresh also took two things away: the 10% anniversary points bonus is gone, and Hyatt transfers dropped to 4:3 — 1,000 Chase points now buy only 750 Hyatt points. Hyatt was the strongest transfer partner, so treat 1.5–2¢ per point as a ceiling rather than a given, and lean on Apple's flat 2% for unbonused spend. Added in exchange: a $100 Chase Travel hotel credit, a $120 TSA PreCheck/Global Entry credit every four years, and a year of Apple TV.
Apple Card
  • 3% Apple, plus Nike, T-Mobile, Panera, Ace Hardware, Exxon/Mobil, Uber, Walgreens
  • 2% anything paid with Apple Pay
  • 1% the physical titanium card
No annual fee. The gap between 2% and 1% is entirely about tapping instead of swiping. Chase takes over as issuer from Goldman Sachs over roughly 24 months starting January 2026 — rewards are unchanged for now, and the 3% list is worth re-checking once the migration lands.
Home Depot Card
  • 0% — no rewards at all
A financing tool, not a rewards card. Its only job is 0% promotional financing on purchases over $299. For everyday Home Depot runs, Citi Custom Cash at 5% beats it outright — provided home improvement is your lane that cycle.
